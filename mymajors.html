My Major: Human Centered Design and Development

The HCDD major at the College of Information Sciences and Technology is a Bachelor of Science program focusing on creating technology applications for human needs. It covers identifying opportunities for tech support, designing user-friendly activities, and evaluating technologies in real-world contexts. Graduates find careers in industry, government, and education, aligning technology with human aspirations. The major combines foundational coursework with specialized studies in social and psychological aspects, usability engineering, user research, and interface design. Graduates excel as HCDD professionals, integrating principles from design thinking, human-computer interaction, and user experience design with software development skills. What is Human-Centered Design and Development? It's the study of identifying, designing, building, and evaluating technologies to enhance people's lives. The field involves understanding user interactions, using methods for effective solution design, and collaborating with users to create impactful products and services.
